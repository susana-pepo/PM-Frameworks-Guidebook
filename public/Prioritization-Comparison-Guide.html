4 Frameworks at a Glance

Each one has a superpower

🍚 RICE Score

R × I × C ÷ E
🕐 Slow📐 High Rigor
The most rigorous. Uses real data for Reach.
Quarterly roadmap planning with 10–15 features. Mature products with analytics data.

⚖️ Value vs Effort

2×2 Matrix
⚡ Fast📐 Low Rigor
The most visual. Plot, see quadrants, decide.
Quick team alignment in 30 min. Backlog grooming. Stakeholder workshops.

📊 Kano Model

Survey → Categorize
🐢 Slowest📐 High Rigor
The most user-centered. Asks users directly.
Product strategy. Understanding what TYPE of features to invest in. Requires user surveys.

🧊 ICE Score

I × C × E
⚡⚡ Fastest📐 Lowest Rigor
The fastest. Three gut-check numbers, multiply, go.
Growth experiments. Rapid triage of 20+ ideas. Early-stage products with no data.

🧠 The one-sentence cheat sheet

ICE when you need speed. Value vs Effort when you need a visual. RICE when you need precision. Kano when you need to understand users.

🤝 Common Pairings

🧊 ICE → 🍚 RICE

ICE filters fast, RICE ranks the survivors rigorously. Best for large backlogs.

📊 Kano → 🍚 RICE

Kano says WHAT TYPE. RICE says WHICH ONES within each type. Partners, not competitors.

⚖️ V/E → 🍚 RICE

V/E for stakeholder alignment, RICE for precise ranking. Visual + rigorous.

📊 Kano → ⚖️ V/E

Kano categorizes, then plot Performance features on V/E grid for quick sprint picks.

⚠️ Don't over-framework

Using all four for every decision is overkill. Most situations need one or two. The pipeline above is for high-stakes, high-ambiguity quarterly planning — not for choosing what to build this sprint.
