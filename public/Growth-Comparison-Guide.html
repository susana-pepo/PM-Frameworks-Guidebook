⚖️ Which Growth Framework?

AARRR vs Growth Loops vs North Star vs Input/Output — when to use each and how they form a growth system.

The Key Difference

These four frameworks form a growth operating system. AARRR diagnoses. North Star focuses. Input/Output operationalizes. Growth Loops compounds.

🏴‍☠️
AARRR
Diagnose: Where are users dropping off?
TIME: 2-4 hours
⭐
North Star
Focus: What ONE metric captures our core value?
TIME: 1-2 days
📥
Input/Output
Operationalize: What levers does each team pull?
TIME: 2-4 hours per team
🔄
Growth Loops
Compound: How does output feed back as input?
TIME: 1-2 days

💡 One Sentence Each

AARRR: "Users drop off at activation." North Star: "We rally around weekly active learners." Input/Output: "Content team publishes 10 lessons/week to drive it." Growth Loops: "Users share progress videos that attract new users."

🔗 The Growth Operating System

🏴‍☠️
Step 1: AARRR — Diagnose
"Biggest drop-off is activation: 60% of signups never complete a lesson."
⬇️
⭐
Step 2: North Star — Focus
"Our North Star is Weekly Active Learners who complete 3+ lessons."
⬇️
📥
Step 3: Input/Output — Operationalize
"Growth team: new activated users. Content: lessons/wk. Product: onboarding rate. Counter: lesson quality."
⬇️
🔄
Step 4: Growth Loops — Compound
"Design a social proof loop: milestone → share video → friends discover → sign up → hit milestone → share."

🎯 For PM Interviews

Show the system: "I'd start by mapping the AARRR funnel to find the biggest drop-off, define a North Star Metric around the value moment, decompose it into input metrics each team owns, and design a growth loop so the product compounds organically." This sentence shows Growth PM-level thinking.
