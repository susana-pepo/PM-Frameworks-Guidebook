🔨 Shape Up

Basecamp's alternative to Scrum: 6-week cycles with appetite-based scoping. Shape before you build, bet instead of plan, ship or kill.

⚡

One-liner: Shape Up is a product development framework where senior people shape problems into bounded pitches, leadership bets on the best ones, and small teams build in fixed 6-week cycles. No backlogs. No estimation. Created at Basecamp by Ryan Singer.

Why Shape Up?

The problems it solves

Scrum's Pain Points That Shape Up Addresses

📋 Endless backlog: In Scrum, the backlog grows forever and creates guilt. Shape Up has no backlog. If an idea isn't pitched and bet on, it dies. Good ideas come back.
⏱️ 2-week sprints too short: Meaningful features rarely fit in 2 weeks. Teams either cut scope too much or carry work across multiple sprints. Shape Up gives 6 weeks — enough to build something substantial.
📊 Estimation theater: Story points are debated endlessly but rarely accurate. Shape Up replaces estimation with appetite: "We're willing to spend 6 weeks on this, no more." The team figures out how to fit it.
🔄 Cycle of grooming: Scrum teams spend hours refining backlog items that may never get built. Shape Up front-loads the thinking in the shaping phase, done by senior people before betting.

🧠 The Core Tradeoff

Scrum: Fixed time (2 weeks), flexible scope (adjust what fits). Shape Up: Fixed time (6 weeks) AND fixed scope (shaped pitch defines boundaries). The variable is quality of execution — teams cut corners on nice-to-haves, not on the core solution.

🎯

The 3 Phases

Shape, Bet, Build

📐 Phase 1: Shape (2-4 weeks, senior people)

A senior PM or designer takes a raw idea and shapes it into a pitch. Not a spec — a pitch is the right level of abstraction: specific enough to be buildable, rough enough to leave room for the team.

Problem: What's the user pain? Why now?
Appetite: How much time is this worth? (Small batch: 2 weeks. Big batch: 6 weeks.)
Solution: Rough sketches (fat-marker sketches, not wireframes). Show the main flow, not every detail.
Rabbit holes: Known risks and how to avoid them. "Don't build a custom audio engine — use existing library."
No-gos: Explicitly what's OUT of scope. "No social features. No custom themes."
🎲 Phase 2: Bet (1 meeting, leadership)

At the betting table, leadership reviews all pitches and decides which ones to fund for the next 6-week cycle. It's a bet: "We believe this pitch is worth 6 weeks of a team's time." Not all pitches get picked. Unpicked pitches don't go into a backlog — they're gone. If the idea is still good next cycle, someone will re-pitch it.

Key rule: If it doesn't ship in 6 weeks, it doesn't get extended. Kill it or re-shape for a future cycle.
🛠️ Phase 3: Build (6 weeks, small team)

A small team (1-3 people: designer + developer) gets the pitch and 6 weeks. They have full autonomy on how to execute. No daily standups with managers. No task assignment from above. The team discovers the detailed design and implementation as they go.

Progress is tracked via hill charts: each scope starts on the "figuring it out" uphill side and moves to the "making it happen" downhill side. This shows real progress, not fake % complete.

Key Concepts

What makes Shape Up unique

🍲 Appetite vs Estimate
Scrum: "How long will this take?" (estimate). Shape Up: "How much time is this worth?" (appetite). Appetite is a business decision, not an engineering guess. Start from budget, not from scope.
🖍️ Fat Marker Sketches
Pitches use rough sketches, not wireframes. A fat marker forces you to show the concept without specifying pixels. Details are the building team's job.
⛰️ Hill Charts
Instead of % complete, each scope is on a hill. Left side = "figuring out" (unknowns). Right side = "making it happen" (execution). Stuck on the uphill? That's the real risk.
❄️ Cooldown (2 weeks)
After each 6-week cycle: 2 weeks of unstructured time. Fix bugs, explore ideas, recharge. No deliverables. This prevents burnout and feeds the next shaping cycle.

🚫 The Circuit Breaker

If a project doesn't ship in 6 weeks, it's killed by default. No extensions. No "just one more week." This forces shaping to be realistic and building teams to make hard tradeoffs. If the idea is still valuable, it can be re-shaped and re-pitched for a future cycle — but it starts fresh.

Real Example

Shape Up for Skoove

🎹 Pitch: "Practice Mode Redesign"

😫 Problem
70% of users who finish a lesson never revisit it to practice. The current "Practice" button just replays the lesson. Users need a dedicated practice experience with tempo control, loop sections, and mistake tracking.
🍲 Appetite: 6-week big batch
This is core to retention. Worth a full cycle. But NOT a full practice suite — we're shaping a focused MVP: tempo slider, section looping, basic mistake highlighting. No social features, no recording.
🖍️ Solution (fat marker)
New "Practice" screen accessible from any completed lesson. Three elements: (1) Tempo slider (50%-150%), (2) Tap-to-loop section markers, (3) Mistake heatmap overlay showing where user struggled. Simple, one-screen experience.
🕳️ Rabbit Holes & No-gos
Rabbit hole: Don't build real-time audio analysis for mistakes — use the existing lesson data to show where errors occurred. No-gos: No recording feature. No sharing. No custom practice playlists. These are future pitches if this works.

💡 Why This Pitch Works

It's bounded (clear scope, explicit no-gos), appetited (worth 6 weeks, no more), and rough enough (the team decides exact UI). The rabbit hole is called out so the team doesn't waste 3 weeks on audio processing. This is what good shaping looks like.

When to Use

Right fit and wrong fit

✅ Use Shape Up When...

  • ✔️ Small, senior teams (2-6 people)
  • ✔️ Projects need more than 2 weeks but less than a quarter
  • ✔️ Backlog is overwhelming and full of stale items
  • ✔️ Teams are capable of autonomous execution
  • ✔️ Leadership wants to make fewer, bigger bets

⛔ Skip Shape Up When...

  • ❌ Large teams that need coordination (10+ people)
  • ❌ Continuous delivery / live ops (use Kanban)
  • ❌ Highly regulated environments needing documentation
  • ❌ Team needs more structure and frequent check-ins
  • ❌ External clients expecting weekly demos (use Scrum)

🔗 Connections

Scrum
Scrum = 2-week sprints, flexible scope. Shape Up = 6-week cycles, shaped scope. Different team sizes and maturity.
Dual Track
Shape Up's "shaping" phase IS a form of discovery — but done by senior individuals, not a discovery squad.
RICE
RICE can inform which pitches to bet on at the betting table — but Shape Up prefers qualitative judgment over scoring.
