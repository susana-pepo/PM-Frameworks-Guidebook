📊 Prioritization Framework

RICE Scoring

Score every product idea with four factors. Build the highest-impact features first.

R× I× C÷ E= Score
⚡

In 15 seconds: RICE scores features by multiplying how many users are affected (Reach) by how much each benefits (Impact) by how sure you are (Confidence), then dividing by total work (Effort). Higher score = build first.

🧩

The 4 Factors

Three multiply up, one divides down

R

Reach

"How many people will this touch?"

Users who'll encounter this feature per quarter. Pull from analytics — don't guess. Same time window for every feature.

📏 users per quarter
Numerator — more reach = higher score
I

Impact

"How much will each person benefit?"

How significantly each user is affected. Use the fixed scale (0.25× to 3×) so everyone scores consistently. Most subjective factor.

📏 multiplier: 0.25, 0.5, 1, 2, or 3
Numerator — more impact = higher score
C

Confidence

"How sure are we about these numbers?"

Your honesty check. Got A/B test data? 100%. Just a hunch? 50%. Prevents half-baked ideas from outranking researched ones.

📏 percentage: 50%, 80%, or 100%
Numerator — more certainty = higher score
E

Effort

"How much total work will this take?"

Person-months across design, eng, QA, docs. The denominator — higher effort pushes the score down. Include everyone.

📏 person-months (design + eng + QA)
Denominator — more effort = lower score

🧠 Memory trick

R × I × C = total value (how many × how much × how sure). Divide by E = cost. It's ROI dressed as an acronym.

When to Use

RICE is powerful but not universal

✅ Use RICE when...

  • You have 10+ competing ideas that need ranking
  • Stakeholders disagree and you need objectivity
  • You have analytics data to estimate Reach
  • Planning a quarterly roadmap across teams
  • You want to reduce gut-feeling bias

🚫 Skip RICE when...

  • Brand-new product with zero usage data
  • Urgent — use Value vs Effort matrix instead
  • Comparing apples to oranges (infra vs feature)
  • Team is small enough to decide in 10 minutes
  • People are gaming scores instead of debating

  7. Sort descending — highest = build first
  8. Discuss — scores start the conversation, not end it

6 Common Pitfalls

Knowing the formula is easy — avoiding these isn't

1

Inflating Impact scores

Everyone thinks their feature is "3× Massive." If 20%+ are 2–3×, you're inflating.

Force a distribution: max 1–2 at 3×, most at 0.5–1×.
2

Guessing Reach

RICE's advantage is Reach uses real data. Guessing defeats the whole point.

Pull from analytics. Can't? Lower Confidence to 50%.
3

Inconsistent time windows

Feature A per month, Feature B per year = meaningless comparison.

Pick one window (quarterly) for every feature in the batch.
4

Underestimating Effort

PMs often only count engineering. Design, QA, docs, migration take months too.

Ask each discipline separately, then sum. Round UP.
5

Scoring alone

One person's scores = one person's biases. RICE works when the group calibrates.

Score together. Divergent votes = the most valuable conversation.
6

Treating scores as final

RICE can't capture strategy, dependencies, or morale. A lower score might be right.

Scores rank the conversation order, not auto-approve the roadmap.
📋

After Scoring

Scores start the conversation, not end it

1

Sort & review

Sort descending. Don't react — just look. Does the order feel roughly right?

Key question: "If I showed the top 5 to our CEO, would anything look wrong?"
2

Apply strategic filters

Check the top 10 against goals, dependencies, and retention risks.

3

Flag overrides & document why

Lower-scored items can jump the queue — but write down why.

Rule: Overriding 20%+ of the ranking? Your inputs need recalibrating.
4

Capacity-check

Map Effort against actual available person-months. Draw the cut line.

5

Commit & communicate

Lock 2–4 items. Share the full list — including what you said no to and why.

Pro move: Save the spreadsheet. Compare estimates to reality next quarter.

Run a Session

90-minute ready-to-use agenda

👥 Who's in the room?

Must have

PM (facilitator), Engineering lead, Design lead

Nice to have

Data analyst, QA lead, stakeholder (observe only)

0–10

Set the stage

Explain RICE. Share spreadsheet. Agree on time window and goal.

10–20

Score Reach

Data analyst leads. Feature by feature: "How many users/quarter?"

20–40

Score Impact & Confidence

Silent vote first (fingers 1–5), reveal, discuss divergences > 1 point.

40–55

Score Effort

Eng lead drives. PM should NOT influence — let builders estimate.

55–70

Calculate & react

Sort live. 2 min silent reading. "What surprises you?"

70–90

Debate & decide

Discuss top 5. Strategic filters. Document overrides. Commit.

💡 Facilitation tips

Silent voting first — prevents anchoring by the loudest voice.

Timebox debates — 3 min max per feature. Park if stuck.

Capture the "why" — add a notes column. Future-you will thank you.

Quick Quiz

5 questions to lock in the learning

1. In the RICE formula, Effort is the...

Correct — Effort is what you divide by. Higher effort = lower score.

2. Reach 10,000 × Impact 2× × Confidence 50% ÷ Effort 2 = ?

10,000 × 2 × 0.5 ÷ 2 = 5,000. The 50% confidence halved the score!

3. The most common mistake with Impact scores?

Everyone thinks their feature is a game-changer. Force a distribution.

4. After sorting by RICE score, what's next?

Scores start the conversation. Check alignment, dependencies, and capacity.

5. When should you NOT use RICE?

No data = no reliable Reach = no advantage over simpler methods.
