📊 Prioritization Framework

ICE Score

The fastest scoring framework. Three gut-check numbers, multiply them, ship.

I× C× E= Score
⚡

In 10 seconds: Rate each feature on three factors — Impact (how much it moves the needle), Confidence (how sure you are), and Ease (how simple to build) — each 1 to 10. Multiply all three. Higher score = build first.

🧩

The 3 Factors

All multiplied — no division, no denominator

I

Impact

"How much will this move the needle?"

The expected effect on your key metric — revenue, retention, engagement, NPS. Pure gut-check on outcome magnitude.

📏 1 (tiny nudge) → 10 (game-changer)
C

Confidence

"How sure am I about Impact and Ease?"

Your honesty multiplier. Got data? Score high. Just a hunch? Score low. Penalizes guesswork, rewards evidence.

📏 1 (wild guess) → 10 (data-proven)
E

Ease

"How simple is this to build?"

The inverse of effort. Higher = easier = better score. Unlike RICE where effort divides, here ease multiplies — easy things get rewarded directly.

📏 1 (months of work) → 10 (afternoon hack)

🧠 Key difference from RICE

ICE has no Reach factor. It doesn't ask "how many users?" — just "how much impact?" This makes it faster but less precise. It also uses Ease (multiply) instead of Effort (divide), so all three factors go in the same direction: higher = better.

ICE vs RICE

When to use which

FormulaI × C × ER × I × C ÷ E
Factors3 (all multiply)4 (one divides)
Scale1–10 eachMixed (users, multiplier, %, months)
Has Reach?❌ No✅ Yes

ObjectivityLower (all gut-check)Higher (Reach uses data)

🎯 The bottom line

ICE = speed. Use it when you need to score 20 ideas in 30 minutes and "roughly right" is good enough.

RICE = precision. Use it when decisions need to hold up to scrutiny and you have analytics data for Reach.

Both together: ICE for first-pass filtering (30 ideas → 10), RICE for final ranking (10 → prioritized roadmap).

When to Use

Speed is ICE's superpower and its limitation

✅ Use ICE when...

  • Scoring growth experiments or A/B test ideas
  • Early-stage product with no analytics data yet
  • You need to triage 20+ ideas in under 30 minutes
  • Hackathon or design sprint — fast prioritization
  • Team is small and everyone has context

🚫 Skip ICE when...

  • Decisions need to hold up to exec scrutiny → use RICE
  • You have analytics data for Reach → RICE is better
  • "How many users does this affect?" is an important question
  • Scores need to be comparable across teams
  • People keep gaming the 1–10 scale

🔗 Where ICE fits in your toolkit

ICE = speed scoring. Great for growth teams running weekly experiments. Use it to rapidly filter, then apply RICE to the top candidates if they need more rigor. Use Value vs Effort for visual alignment. Use Kano to understand feature types.

⚠️

5 Common Pitfalls

ICE is simple — that's what makes it dangerous

1

The 1–10 scale is too subjective

My "7 impact" is your "4 impact." Without anchored definitions, scores are incomparable across people.

Write anchor definitions before scoring. "Impact 8 = moves key metric by 10%+." Share the rubric with everyone.
2

Missing Reach entirely

A feature that impacts 100 users at "Impact 9" and one that impacts 100,000 users at "Impact 9" get the same score. ICE can't tell them apart.

If "how many users?" matters, upgrade to RICE. Or add a mental check: "Is this a niche feature or a broad one?"
3

Confidence inflation

Everyone scores their pet idea Confidence 8. Real confidence requires evidence — not enthusiasm.

Anchor it: 1–3 = no data. 4–6 = qualitative signals. 7–9 = quantitative data. 10 = A/B tested or proven.
4

All scores cluster at 5–7

People avoid extremes. If everything is 5–7 on all three factors, the scores barely differentiate (125 vs 343).

Force spread. Use the full range. If nothing scores below 4 or above 8, challenge the team to be bolder.
5

Using ICE for high-stakes decisions

ICE is a triage tool, not a planning tool. Using it to finalize a quarterly roadmap is like using a sketch to build a bridge.

ICE for first pass. RICE for final decisions. Know when to upgrade your tool.

Run a Session

30-minute speed scoring

👥 Who's in the room?

Must have

PM, Eng lead, anyone who owns the ideas

Nice to have

Designer, data analyst, growth marketer

🧰 Before you start

Spreadsheet with features listed. Anchor definitions for 1–10 scales posted where everyone can see them. Agree on the key metric you're optimizing for.

0–5

Set context

Explain ICE. Share anchor definitions. "Today we're scoring against [key metric]."

5–10

Silent scoring

Everyone independently scores all features: I, C, E (1–10 each). No discussion. 5 minutes, timer running.

10–20

Reveal & average

Go feature by feature. Everyone reveals. Average the scores. Flag items where scores diverge by 3+ points — discuss those briefly.

20–30

Sort & commit

Calculate I×C×E. Sort descending. Pick the top 3–5 to execute. Done.

💡 Why 30 minutes works

ICE sessions are fast because the framework is fast. Don't let it expand to fill an hour. The whole point is speed. If debates take longer than 2 minutes per feature, you probably need RICE instead.

Quick Quiz

5 questions to lock it in

1. What does the "E" in ICE stand for?

Ease — the inverse of Effort. Higher Ease = easier = higher score. Unlike RICE where you divide by Effort, ICE multiplies by Ease.

2. Impact 8 × Confidence 3 × Ease 7 = ?

8 × 3 × 7 = 168. Notice how Confidence 3 (gut feel) crushed what could have been a 560 (8×10×7). Evidence matters.

3. What's the biggest thing ICE is missing compared to RICE?

No Reach factor. ICE can't distinguish between a feature affecting 100 users and one affecting 100,000. If that matters, use RICE.

4. When is ICE the BEST choice over RICE?

ICE's speed makes it perfect for growth teams triaging experiments weekly. 30 minutes, 20 ideas, done.

5. Everyone on your team scored every feature between 5 and 7. What's the problem?

Clustering at 5–7 means the scores barely spread (125 vs 343). Challenge the team to use the full range — some things really are a 2 or a 9.
