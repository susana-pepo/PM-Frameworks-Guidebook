The Core Idea

Your product is a leaky bucket

Why AARRR Matters

Most teams look at vanity metrics: total signups, page views, downloads. AARRR forces you to look at the full user lifecycle and find where people drop off. It's not about getting more users in — it's about fixing the leaks so more users make it through.

The name "Pirate Metrics" comes from the sound: A-A-R-R-R. Dave McClure created it to give startups a simple mental model for growth that isn't just "get more traffic."

🚩 The Biggest Mistake

Spending money on acquisition (ads, marketing) when your activation or retention is broken. It's like pouring water into a bucket with a hole in the bottom. Fix the hole first, then pour more water.

🧠 Memory Trick

Think of it as a pirate ship voyage. Acquisition = recruiting crew. Activation = first successful voyage. Retention = crew stays aboard. Revenue = finding treasure. Referral = crew recruits more pirates. AARRR! 🏴‍☠️

The 5 Stages

What happens at each level

📣 1. Acquisition

Question: How do users find your product?

Channels: SEO, paid ads, social media, app store, partnerships, word of mouth, content marketing. The goal isn't max volume — it's finding channels that bring quality users who actually convert.

Key insight: Measure cost per acquired user BY CHANNEL, not just total.
✨ 2. Activation

Question: Do users have a great first experience?

This is the "aha moment" — when a user first experiences real value. For Trala: playing their first song with real-time feedback. For Slack: sending their first message and getting a reply. If users don't hit the aha moment, nothing else matters.

Key insight: Define YOUR aha moment. Remove every friction between signup and that moment.
🔄 3. Retention

Question: Do users come back?

The most important stage. Retention is the foundation everything else is built on. If users don't come back, acquisition is waste and revenue is impossible. Measure D1, D7, D30 retention (Day 1, 7, 30 after first use).

Key insight: Retention is the KING metric. Fix this before anything else.
💰 4. Revenue

Question: Do users pay (and how much)?

Monetization: subscriptions, in-app purchases, ads, upsells. Key metrics: conversion rate (free to paid), ARPU (average revenue per user), LTV (lifetime value), and critically — LTV > CAC (you make more from a user than it costs to acquire them).

Key insight: Revenue only works if retention is solid. Retained users = compounding revenue.
📢 5. Referral

Question: Do users tell others?

Organic growth: NPS (Net Promoter Score), viral coefficient (each user brings K new users), referral programs, social sharing. A viral coefficient > 1 means exponential growth. Most products are 0.1-0.5 (every 10 users bring 1-5 new ones).

Key insight: Referral is free acquisition. But users only refer products they love (= retained users).

💡 The Priority Order

Most people think AARRR is top-down (fix acquisition first). It's actually middle-out. Fix Retention first, then Activation, then Revenue, then Acquisition, then Referral. Why? There's no point acquiring users who won't stick around.

🔍 The LTV:CAC Ratio

The single most important business health metric. LTV (Lifetime Value) = total revenue from a user over their lifetime. CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) = what you spend to get them.

LTV:CAC < 1
Losing money on every user. Stop spending.
LTV:CAC 1-3
Sustainable but thin margins. Optimize.
LTV:CAC > 3
Healthy. Can invest more in acquisition.

Real Example

AARRR for Skoove / Trala

🎹 Skoove's Pirate Funnel

📣 Acquisition
Channels: App Store search ("learn piano"), YouTube ads, Instagram, partnerships with keyboard brands. Metric: ~$3-5 CPI (Cost Per Install). Best channel: App Store organic (free, high intent).
✨ Activation
Aha moment: Playing a recognizable song with real-time feedback for the first time. Metric: % who complete first song lesson within 24h. Blocker: Users who download but never connect their instrument.
🔄 Retention
Reality: Music learning apps have ~70% churn in first month. Levers: Practice reminders, streak mechanics, progressive difficulty, "songs you know." Key metric: D7 and D30 lesson completion rate.
💰 Revenue
Model: Freemium to subscription ($9.99/mo or $59.99/yr). Conversion: Free trial to paid ~3-5%. Key lever: Hitting the paywall after aha moment, not before.
📢 Referral
Organic: Users recording themselves playing and sharing on social. Built-in: "Invite a friend, get 1 month free" referral program. NPS: Likely strongest among users who hit 30-day milestone.

🔍 Where's the Leak?

For most music learning apps, the biggest leak is Retention. Users install (acquisition OK), try a lesson (activation OK), but don't come back after week 1. The fix isn't more marketing — it's better habit formation, progress visibility, and making practice feel rewarding, not like homework.

When to Use

Right situations and wrong ones

✅ Use AARRR When...

  • ✔️ Diagnosing where growth is stuck
  • ✔️ Prioritizing what to work on next
  • ✔️ PM interview asks about growth strategy
  • ✔️ Reporting metrics to leadership
  • ✔️ Building a growth team's dashboard

⛔ Skip AARRR When...

  • ❌ Pre-product-market-fit (too early for funnel optimization)
  • ❌ B2B enterprise with long sales cycles (use sales funnel instead)
  • ❌ You need feature-level prioritization (use RICE/ICE)
  • ❌ You need strategic direction (use Playing to Win)
  • ❌ You need user insights (use JTBD)

🔗 Connections to Other Frameworks

North Star
Funnel → Focus: AARRR shows all stages. North Star picks the ONE metric that matters most right now.
Growth Loops
Linear → Circular: AARRR is a funnel (linear). Growth Loops show how output feeds back as input (compounding).
RICE
Find → Fix: AARRR finds the leaky stage. RICE prioritizes which features to build to fix it.
OKRs
Funnel → Goals: AARRR metrics become Key Results in your OKRs. "Improve D30 retention from 8% to 15%."
