🛤️ Dual Track Agile

Run discovery and delivery in parallel. Research what to build next while building what you've already validated. Popularized by Marty Cagan / Jeff Patton.

⚡

One-liner: Two parallel tracks run simultaneously: the Discovery Track (PM + designer research, test, validate ideas) feeds validated items into the Delivery Track (engineers build and ship). Discovery stays 1-2 sprints ahead of delivery.

The Problem

Why one track isn't enough

The Feature Factory Trap

In many teams, the PO writes stories, engineers build them, and nobody validates if users actually need them. The result: a feature factory — shipping features that don't move metrics.

The root cause: discovery and delivery happen sequentially. First you research (weeks), then you build (weeks), then you learn it was wrong. Dual Track solves this by running both in parallel.

❌ Single Track
Research → (wait) → Spec → (wait) → Build → (wait) → Ship → Learn it was wrong. Months wasted.
✅ Dual Track
Discovery researches Sprint N+1 while delivery builds Sprint N. By the time a validated idea reaches engineers, risk is already reduced. No wasted effort.

🧠 Memory Trick

Think of a restaurant kitchen. The chef (discovery) is always prepping the next dish while the line cooks (delivery) are plating the current one. Both happen simultaneously. Discovery = prep. Delivery = cooking.

🔄

The Two Tracks

How they work together

🔍 Discovery Track
Who: PM + Designer (+ sometimes 1 engineer for feasibility)
Goal: Answer 4 risks BEFORE building: Is it valuable? Usable? Feasible? Viable?
Cadence: Continuous. Multiple experiments per week. Fast, cheap, disposable.
Output: Validated backlog items ready for delivery.
🛠️ Delivery Track
Who: Full engineering team + designer + QA
Goal: Build, test, and ship validated items reliably.
Cadence: 2-week sprints (Scrum) or continuous flow (Kanban).
Input: Only validated items from discovery.

🔑 The Handoff Point

An idea moves from discovery to delivery when it passes all 4 risk tests. The "validated backlog" sits between the two tracks. Keep 1-2 sprints worth of ready items — enough buffer so delivery never starves, but not so much that research gets stale.

Discovery Toolkit

The 4 risks and how to test them

The 4 Product Risks (Marty Cagan)

❤️ Value Risk
Will users want this?
Test: User interviews, fake door tests, landing page MVPs
🧩 Usability Risk
Can users figure it out?
Test: Prototype usability tests (5 users), Wizard of Oz
⚙️ Feasibility Risk
Can we build this?
Test: Technical spike (1-2 days), architecture review
💰 Viability Risk
Does it work for the business?
Test: Stakeholder review, legal check, unit economics

⏱️ Discovery Timebox Rule

Each experiment should take hours to days, not weeks. A quick prototype tested with 5 users gives 80% of insight at 5% of the cost of building the real thing.

Interview: 1 hour
Figma prototype: 1-2 days
Usability test: 1 day
Tech spike: 1-2 days
Fake door: 2-3 hours

💡 The 80% Rule

You don't need to eliminate ALL risk — reduce it enough to make a confident bet. If 4/5 users complete the task in your prototype, that's enough. Ship it, measure, iterate. Discovery de-risks. Delivery validates at scale.

Real Example

Dual Track at Trala

🎻 Sprint 15-16: Two Tracks Running

🔍 Discovery (Sprint 15) — "Should we add duets?"
Week 1: Interviewed 8 users. 6/8 want to play with someone. But "duets" means different things — recording vs live.
Week 1: Feasibility spike — real-time sync = 6+ months. Backing track = 3-4 weeks.
Week 2: Figma prototype of "Play Along." Tested with 5 users. 4/5 completed flow. Redesigned confusing sync button.
Outcome: ✅ Validated. Moved to delivery backlog for Sprint 17.
🛠️ Delivery (Sprint 15) — Building validated items
Building: Practice mode redesign (validated Sprint 13). Tempo slider + section looping.
Building: Onboarding improvement (validated Sprint 14). Reduced from 7 to 3 steps.
Shipped: Both features done. Practice mode → A/B test. Onboarding → 100% rollout.

🔗 See the Pattern?

Discovery Sprint 13-14 → Delivery Sprint 15. Discovery Sprint 15 → Delivery Sprint 17. The two tracks overlap like train tracks. Engineers never wait. PMs never stop learning.

When to Use

Right fit and alternatives

✅ Use Dual Track When...

  • ✔️ You have a dedicated PM + designer for discovery
  • ✔️ High uncertainty — building wrong things is costly
  • ✔️ You want to stop being a feature factory
  • ✔️ Team is mature enough for parallel workstreams
  • ✔️ B2C products with accessible users for testing

⛔ Skip When...

  • ❌ Very small team where PM also does delivery work
  • ❌ Requirements are clear and well-validated
  • ❌ Maintenance/bug-fix phase (use Kanban)
  • ❌ No access to users for testing
  • ❌ Team can't handle cognitive load of two tracks
